Optimization goal: minimize hardware area.

Implement an IEEE-754 single-precision floating-point adder with a strong emphasis on reducing logic area.

Design guidelines:
	•	Favor resource sharing over parallelism, even if it increases combinational depth.
	•	Use a single shared alignment and normalization datapath instead of duplicating logic.
	•	Prefer serial or iterative-style logic for mantissa alignment and normalization where possible.
	•	Avoid speculative or parallel fast paths.
	•	Keep the control logic simple and compact.

The design should be fully combinational except for the top-level interface, and correctness is required, but timing is not the primary concern.
